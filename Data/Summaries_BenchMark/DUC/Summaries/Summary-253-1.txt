The Reagan administration has moved to deal with the worsening drought
by appointing an interagency group and authorizing farmers in dry
counties to harvest hay on Conservation Reserve Program land.
Thirteen states are now involved.  A White House spokesman said the
drought would drive up food prices, but not have overall implications
for the economy.  There was speculation that the export enhancement
program would be curbed and embargos placed on soybean exports.
Bankers are concerned about payments on farm loans and fewer loan
applications.  Farm equipment makers fear sales will drop, while
realtors fear an end to the land boom.
