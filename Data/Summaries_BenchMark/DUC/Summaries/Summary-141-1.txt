The president of Brazil blames the industrial nations for perpetuating
the poverty of the developing nations.

In West Berlin, the finance ministers of 22 developed or developing
countries demanded action to help Third World countries with their 1.2
trillion dollar debt.

Brazil, the world's most indebted nation with 121 billion in foreign
loans, has been among the most vociferous lobbyists for debt
restructuring and write-offs.

There is an imbalance in trade. One group is selling its wares cheaply
and buying those of others dearly.

The seven key industrialized nations have approved a plan to aid the
world's poorest countries.
